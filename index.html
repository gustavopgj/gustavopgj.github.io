Work in Progress

"Credit and Economic Activity: The Role of Government-Owned Banks " with Felipe Netto and José Renato Haas Ornelas

We use a large-scale change in lending policy from state-owned banks in Brazil to understand their effect on access to credit and economic activity at both the micro and macro levels. Starting in March 2012, the Brazilian government attempted to use state-owned banks to induce more lending at lower prices from both state-owned and non-state banks. Specifically, state-owned unexpectedly increased their credit levels by 20% and reduced interest rates by 7 percentage points when compared with private banks. Using matched detailed administrative data on firms and loans, we theoretically and empirically explore the effect of this policy.
