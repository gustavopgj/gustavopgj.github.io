Work in Progress

"Credit and Economic Activity: The Role of Public Banks " with Felipe Netto and José Renato Haas Ornelas

There is a limited understanding of the role of the public banks in affecting access to credit and economic activity at a macro level. We use a large-scale change in lending policy from public banks in Brazil to understand their role. Starting in March 2012, the Brazilian government attempted to use state-owned banks to induce more lending at lower prices from both state-owned and non-state banks. Specifically, state-owned unexpectedly increased their credit levels by 20% and reduced interest rates by 7 percentage points when compared with private banks. Using matched detailed administrative data on firms and loans, we theoretically and empirically explore the effect of this policy.
